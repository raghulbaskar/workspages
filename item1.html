Franco

  • digital
  • fashion & ecommerce
  • 2018

GOAL

The name "Franco" is a French word related to France. As this website is for a Fashion Ecommerce for redefining fashion, situated in France, we came with this name which rightly justifies the very existence of the company. It's a multipurpose e-commerce website where consumers can keep track of the latest trends in the fashion industry and find a variety of accessories to define their styles and create trends.

STO

RY.

As the name reached seas and beyond, they felt the need to create a website, so we came to their rescue. All franco needed was a website which is as trendy as its products and satisfying market needs. So we came up with a responsive design, supportive backend, and a simple UX that can engage customers to deliver its reach worldwide.

Now that they have a website that is customized according to their service, goods, and trends, we are just waiting for the establishment of Franco as an industry leader to reach and influence the masses.

PHOTOGRAPHY

We worked with Franco to create realistic photos that truly reflect the styles and trends of the industry. Photos are the result of the hard work of models and photographers who have helped to bridge the gap between online stores and actual stores with the originality of the content.

UX.

We set two main UX goals for the project: making the content more enjoyable and enhancing user orientation by simplifying the navigation. We completely rebuilt the site architecture, introducing easier navigation and a clean category distribution. We recategorized, grouped, and simplified the online catalog into two main categories – Uniforms and Equipment. Technologically we built a catalog system that can present an unlimited number of categories, which is easy for the client to edit and easy for users to navigate.

DES

IGN.

To cover all necessary design assets for the site, we provided comprehensive web design and consulting services as well as a photo shoot. Broken grid; generous white space; and bold, skewed and soft fonts that suggest an interplay between motion and stability.
